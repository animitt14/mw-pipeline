How these allocations are built
Universe. Only active (open) offerings with at least $10k of shares still available; existing-user-only offerings are excluded. Availability = unsold shares × $20/share.
Risk Tolerance (1–10). The slider sets a target risk level. Each work is scored on a 1–6 risk scale (Conservative→Speculative); a tolerance of 1 centers picks on the lowest-risk works and 10 on the highest, sliding smoothly in between.
How many works. The book is broadest in the middle of the scale (~15 works) and more concentrated at the extremes (~9 works), so a higher risk tolerance means fewer, larger positions.
Balance. Among works that fit the target risk, selection favors variety across gender, living vs. deceased, and region — so the mix stays diversified rather than clustering. This is a visual tilt, not a hard rule.
Weighting. “Made in Japan II” (Basquiat) is held in every portfolio and overweighted to roughly twice a normal position (capped at 25%). All remaining works split the rest evenly, so weights total 100%.
Dollars. When an Amount is entered, each weight is converted to dollars and rounded to whole $20 shares (no fractional ownership). Blended appreciation = the allocation-weighted average of each work’s repeat-sales appreciation.
